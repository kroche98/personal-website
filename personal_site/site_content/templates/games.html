Puzzles & Games

Rubik’s Cube

The Rubik’s Cube is a combination puzzle which was invented in 1974 by Ernő Rubik. It has inspired numerous variants. To see a web version of the puzzle, click here.

The cube has a deep mathematical structure related to group theory. To see my senior thesis which explores these topics and describes the “Cubix Tube” variant, click here.

TwixT

TwixT is a two-person strategy board-game released in the 1960s as part of the 3M bookshelf game series. Players take turns placing pegs and barriers, attempting to complete an unbroken barrier from one side of the board to the other. It was one of my favorite games when I was growing up.

To play a web version of the game, click here.

Fifteen Puzzle

The Fifteen Puzzle is a classic sliding puzzle. The original version contains 15 numbered tiles in a 4×4 grid. Other variants involve different-sized grids or replace the numbered tiles with pieces of a picture.

To play a web version of the game, click here.
